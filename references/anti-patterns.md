# Conditional Anti-Patterns

Use anti-patterns to diagnose unjustified defaults. Never ban a component merely because it is common.

## Generic opening

**Signal:** Interchangeable headline, paragraph, primary button, and decorative image introduce the page without revealing specific value.

**Failure condition:** The structure appears before the audience understands the offering, context, or evidence.

**Valid use:** A conventional hero is effective when the audience needs rapid orientation and the content is specific, credible, and well prioritized.

**Replace with:** Define the opening objective, then choose the simplest structure that achieves it.

## Equal-card flattening

**Signal:** Three or four identical cards give unrelated or unequal ideas the same weight.

**Failure condition:** Component symmetry replaces information hierarchy.

**Valid use:** Cards are appropriate for genuinely parallel choices or comparable objects.

**Replace with:** Rank ideas, let one dominate when necessary, and vary presentation according to meaning.

## Decorative metrics

**Signal:** Large numbers create credibility without context, source, or decision value.

**Failure condition:** The number signals success but proves nothing relevant to the audience.

**Valid use:** A metric is useful when its definition, source, and consequence are clear.

**Replace with:** Add context, show change, connect the number to a case, or remove it.

## Default SaaS translation

**Signal:** Every offering becomes a dashboard, glowing interface, floating card, blue-purple gradient, or software-style feature grid.

**Failure condition:** The visual world contradicts how the product or service exists and creates value.

**Valid use:** Software patterns are appropriate for software behavior that audiences need to understand.

**Replace with:** Draw from the brand's real environments, objects, processes, people, and evidence.

## Adjective-to-style shortcut

**Signal:** Premium becomes black; technology becomes blue; sustainable becomes green; friendly becomes rounded; bold becomes oversized type.

**Failure condition:** A broad word selects a style before its brand-specific meaning is resolved.

**Replace with:** Ask what creates the claimed quality and express that source.

## Explanation without evidence

**Signal:** Long copy and repeated claims compensate for weak demonstration, examples, or proof.

**Failure condition:** The audience is asked to believe what could be observed or substantiated.

**Valid use:** Explanation is necessary for complex, technical, regulated, inaccessible, or search-dependent content.

**Replace with:** Pair claims with product behavior, cases, data, process, imagery, comparison, or precise detail.

## Effect-first design

**Signal:** Gradients, glass, glow, texture, 3D, scroll effects, or unusual typography define the concept.

**Failure condition:** Removing the effect removes the perceived quality because structure, content, and evidence are weak.

**Valid use:** Effects can express an established brand world or clarify hierarchy and state.

**Replace with:** Validate the design in a reduced visual form before restoring purposeful effects.

## Decorative motion

**Signal:** Continuous fades, parallax, floating objects, or rotation add activity without revealing information.

**Failure condition:** Motion competes with the offering, delays tasks, or ignores reduced-motion needs.

**Valid use:** Motion explains change, sequence, cause, spatial relationship, or feedback.

**Replace with:** State what the audience learns from the motion. Remove it if the answer is nothing.

## Forced memorable moment

**Signal:** Every page receives a cinematic climax regardless of task.

**Failure condition:** Spectacle interrupts comparison, support, documentation, checkout, or other task-focused experiences.

**Valid use:** A distinctive moment can anchor a high-value narrative page when it reinforces understanding.

**Replace with:** Match intensity to the page's purpose.

## First-result convergence

**Signal:** The first polished output becomes the direction because it is complete and easy to discuss.

**Failure condition:** Production quality hides unresolved assumptions and unconsidered alternatives.

**Replace with:** Compare strategic hypotheses before investing in polish.

## Untraceable invention

**Signal:** The agent introduces a new color language, imagery style, interaction, claim, or priority without source or explanation.

**Failure condition:** A consequential decision is silently made during execution.

**Replace with:** Trace the choice to the foundation, evidence, constraint, or an explicit provisional assumption.

## Interchangeability test

Temporarily remove the logo, brand name, and signature product image. Ask:

- Could this page credibly belong to several unrelated brands?
- Which remaining elements encode this brand's world, value, behavior, or point of view?
- Is familiarity serving clarity, or replacing identity?

Do not demand novelty everywhere. Require specificity where it affects recognition, understanding, or trust.
